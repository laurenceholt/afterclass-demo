# The Demo Scenario: A Week in G8 Math

**Setting**: Grade 8 math class, transitioning from Unit 3 (slope, y = mx + b) into Unit 4 (Linear Equations). The teacher and an AI agent ("Afterclass") collaborate through a chat sidebar while tasks move across a kanban board. The week runs Mon Nov 3 – Fri Nov 7.

---

## Monday, Nov 3

**Teacher** kicks things off: she gave the Unit 3 assessment on Friday and is jumping straight into Unit 4 Lesson 1 today. She asks the agent to analyze results while she teaches.

The agent creates 4 initial tasks:
- **Analyze Unit 3 Assessment Results** (high priority)
- **OKO Group: Writing Equations from Tables** — Maya, DeShawn, Imani practice writing y = mx + b from tables via OKO small-group tool
- **Extra Practice: Marcus** — he confuses slope and y-intercept; 1-on-1 on Podsie
- **Extra Practice: Aaliyah** — Grade 7 gap on proportional relationships (y = kx); 1-on-1 on Podsie

Teacher approves all four. Assessment analysis and the OKO equations group move to In Progress.

**After Lesson 1** (Writing Equivalent Equations): Teacher reports most kids got equivalence but a third can't explain *why* — they want to memorize steps. Agent suggests using the balance metaphor in tomorrow's hanger diagram lesson.

**OKO group finishes**: Maya 92%, DeShawn 85% (still confuses initial value with first y-value), Imani 88%. Card moves to Done.

---

## Tuesday, Nov 4

**Assessment results come in** with four key findings:
1. 58% struggled to calculate slope from two points
2. 6 students can't reliably write y = mx + b from a graph
3. Aaliyah, Jayden, and 3 others still shaky on proportional relationships (Grade 7 gap)
4. Darius and Zara aced everything — ready for extension

Assessment moves to Review. Agent recommends 3 new tasks:
- **OKO Small Group: Calculating Slope** — Jayden, Aisha, Carlos
- **Revise Warm-Ups to Practice Slope** — use coteach.ai for Lessons 3–4 (depends on assessment finishing)
- **Challenge: Equation Error Analysis** — extension for Darius and Zara

**Teacher's call**: Approves the OKO slope group and lesson revision. **Rejects** the challenge card — wants to see how Darius and Zara do in class first.

OKO slope group and Marcus's Podsie practice move to In Progress.

**After Lesson 2** (Keeping the Equation Balanced / Hanger Diagrams): Hangers were a hit, but Jayden and others kept wanting to "solve" without explaining the balance. Marcus was lost — teacher is glad he's getting Podsie time.

**Marcus breakthrough**: He was reading the coefficient of x as the y-intercept. After three graphs on Podsie, he got 11/12 correct. Marcus → Done.

---

## Wednesday, Nov 5

**OKO slope group wraps up**: Aisha and Carlos can now calculate slope. Carlos had a 40% engagement increase — he started pushing the other two to prove their answers. Jayden still sometimes reverses rise and run (flagged for follow-up). Card → Done.

Aaliyah starts Podsie. Assessment analysis moves to Done, **unlocking** the lesson revision (dependency resolved) — revision moves to In Progress via coteach.ai.

**After Lesson 3** (Balanced Moves): Teacher reports this was TOUGH. When moving from hanger diagrams to actual equations, about half the class couldn't connect the moves. The hanger-to-equation transition is the real sticking point.

**The agent proposes a research-backed intervention**: A contrasting cases game (citing Bransford & Schwartz, 1999) where students see a hanger side-by-side with an equation and decide if they match, run in Brisk for live progress tracking.

Teacher loves it: "Build it. And have coteach.ai fit it into the Lesson 3 warm-up."

Two new tasks created and approved:
- **Build Hanger Diagram Game** (Brisk)
- **Assign Game to Class** (depends on game completing)

Agent resurfaces the challenge card for Darius and Zara. Teacher approves it this time.

**Aaliyah completes Podsie**: Can now distinguish y = kx from y = mx + b. Grade 7 gap closed. → Done.

Challenge and game move to In Progress.

---

## Thursday, Nov 6

coteach.ai finishes warm-up revisions (with slope practice + game integration). Hanger game is built. Both move to Review.

Agent proposes 2 more tasks:
- **Analyze Practice for Common Bugs** — review cool-downs for error patterns
- **Create Video: Slope from Two Points** — explainer video for home viewing (depends on bug analysis)

Teacher approves both. Bug analysis moves to In Progress.

**After Lesson 4**: Teacher reports it went WAY better. Both the coteach warm-up changes AND the contrasting cases game were a hit. DeShawn was explaining to his table partner why a hanger *didn't* match the equation.

**Bug analysis finds the top 3 errors**:
1. Confusing slope/y-intercept when equation is y = b + mx (41%)
2. Calculating slope as run/rise instead of rise/run (28%)
3. Not recognizing negative slope means the line goes down (19%)

Bug analysis → Done, **unlocking** the video (dependency resolved). Video → In Progress. Teacher asks the video to show both equation forms (y = mx + b and y = b + mx) side by side.

Game → Done.

---

## Friday, Nov 7

Video is rendered with both equation forms. Darius and Zara crushed the error analysis — found all 4 errors in Lin's solution path. Video → Review, Challenge → Done.

Teacher approves everything: warm-up revisions → Done, video → Done. Assigns the slope video as weekend homework. Hanger game assigned as extra practice → In Progress → Done.

**Week-end summary**:
- Lessons 1–4 taught
- Unit 3 gaps identified — slope from two points was the biggest
- Both OKO groups closed key gaps (Carlos had a 40% engagement jump)
- Marcus and Aaliyah caught up via Podsie 1-on-1s
- Darius & Zara completed error analysis extension
- Slope video assigned for weekend
- Contrasting cases game assigned for extra practice
- 11 total tasks, all complete

Teacher: "The contrasting cases game and the coteach revisions were game changers — DeShawn explaining why a hanger didn't match the equation was the highlight. See you Monday for Lesson 5!"

---

## Key dynamics on display

- **Teacher stays in the loop**: every task requires approval before execution. She rejects the challenge card on Tuesday, then approves it on Wednesday after seeing the students in class.
- **AI responds to what happens in the classroom**: the game is born from the teacher's report that Lesson 3 was tough — it's not pre-planned.
- **Dependencies chain naturally**: assessment → revision → warm-ups; bug analysis → video.
- **Multiple tools orchestrated**: OKO (small group), Podsie (1-on-1 retrieval), Brisk (game delivery), coteach.ai (lesson revision).
- **Individual students tracked by name**: Marcus's slope/y-intercept confusion, Aaliyah's Grade 7 gap, Carlos's engagement spike, Jayden's rise/run reversal.
